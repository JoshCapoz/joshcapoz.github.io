I'm currently working through CompTIA Security+, targeting an October 2026 exam date. I wanted to write down where things actually stand a few months in, partly because most study journey posts either come from people who haven't started full time work yet, or get written retrospectively once someone's already passed. This is neither, just an honest snapshot from the middle of it.

## How I'm actually fitting it in

I stick to a rigid study block carved out every day. Realistically it's whatever free time comes up: an hour or two in the evening after work when I've got the energy for it, and a longer stretch on the weekend, usually a couple of hours. It's most likely why it's taking me months compared to a couple weeks of proper study to achieve this certification. Some weeks that adds up to a decent amount of study time, other weeks work is heavier and it doesn't. I used to feel like that made me "behind," but it's a more honest description of how most people actually study around a full-time job than the "two hours every single day, no exceptions" plans that tend to get posted online.

## What's actually surprised me: encryption

Going in, I assumed cryptography would be one of the more straightforward domains, mostly because "encryption keeps data safe" is such a simple mental model day to day. Actually sitting with the material has been a different story. The distinction between symmetric and asymmetric encryption is easy enough on the surface, but once you get into where each one actually gets used, key exchange, hashing versus encryption, and how trust actually gets established through a certificate chain and PKI, it stops being a single concept and turns into a whole layered system with a lot of moving parts.

It's the kind of topic where I can explain the basic idea confidently, then get asked a slightly more specific question and realise I don't actually know the mechanism underneath it yet. That's been a useful signal for where to slow down rather than just moving on to the next objective.

## What's felt more familiar

Not everything has been equally new. Since I already work in security day to day, some domains map fairly closely onto things I already have a working intuition for, even if the exam wants more formal terminology than I'd normally use out loud. Cryptography hasn't had that shortcut. It's been more of a "sit down and actually learn this properly" domain than an "I basically already know this" one.

I love that though. It helps keep me engaged and interested ready to learn a new concept. Because once I gain that confidence that's another skill I can add to my list. It's exactly the kind of hard work I'm looking for.

## Where that leaves things

Still on track for October, still chipping away in whatever time is actually available rather than the time I'd ideally have. I'll probably write a follow up once I've sat the exam, either way it goes.
