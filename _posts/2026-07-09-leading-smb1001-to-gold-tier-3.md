## What SMB1001 actually is

SMB1001 is a cyber security certification framework built specifically for small and medium
businesses, structured in tiers, Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, each adding more controls on
top of the last. It's a lighter-weight, more achievable framework than something like ISO 27001,
but the principles underneath it (documented controls, evidence, gap analysis, ongoing review)
are the same ones ISO 27001 is built on. A lot of the small businesses I work with use SMB1001 as
a realistic starting point rather than trying to jump straight to ISO 27001 with no security
program in place at all.

## Why we did it for ourselves first

We'd been implementing SMB1001 for clients for a while before we went through the process of
getting our own company certified. Going through it ourselves changed how I think about it, it's
one thing to tell a client what controls they need, it's a different thing to actually hold the
certification yourself and be accountable for the evidence when it's your own environment being
assessed.

## Getting to Gold (Tier 3)

The process came down to three things done properly: gap analysis against the framework's
requirements, remediation of whatever controls weren't in place yet, and building an evidence
trail that would actually hold up if someone came and checked it rather than just ticking a box
and hoping.

- **Gap analysis:** going through the Gold-tier requirements line by line against what we
  actually had in place *(EDIT: mention specific gap areas if you can share them, e.g. MFA
  coverage, patch cadence, backup testing, whatever was genuinely a gap)*.
- **Remediation:** closing those gaps, prioritising the ones that mattered most for actual risk
  reduction rather than just whatever was quickest to tick off.
- **Evidence:** documenting controls in a way that's audit-ready on an ongoing basis, not just
  scrambled together right before an assessment.

## Rolling it out to clients

Having gone through Gold ourselves made the client-facing side of this work sharper. Three clients
have now reached Bronze (Tier 1) certification with me leading that work, and I understand the
framework differently having sat on the other side of it, I know what evidence actually gets
scrutinised and what a rushed gap analysis looks like versus a proper one.

## What this taught me about GRC

This is the part of my job that's pointed me toward wanting to move further into governance, risk
and compliance long term. Technical controls are only half of it, the other half is the discipline
of documenting, evidencing, and being able to defend why a control exists and whether it's
actually working. SMB1001 is a smaller, more approachable framework than ISO 27001 or the ACSC
Essential 8, but the underlying skill, translating a framework's requirements into real, evidenced
controls, is the same skill either way.
